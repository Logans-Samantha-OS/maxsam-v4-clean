## OVERVIEW

Fort Bend County (Sugar Land/Richmond area) handles excess funds through the District Clerk's Office. The county provides an "Excess Proceeds from Tax Sale" report through their Research/Resource Information section. When properties sell at tax foreclosure for more than the amount owed, surplus funds are held for eligible claimants.

## EXCESS PROCEEDS REPORT

Fort Bend County provides a downloadable **"Excess Proceeds from Tax Sale"** PDF document through the District Clerk's Research/Resource Information section.

**Check this report first to identify potential claims.**

---

## DEADLINE TO FILE CLAIM

**2 YEARS** from the date of the tax foreclosure sale.

If no petition is filed within this timeframe, funds are distributed to the taxing units.

---

## STEP-BY-STEP CLAIM PROCESS

### Step 1: Request Excess Proceeds Report
- Call District Clerk's Office at (281) 342-3411
- Or visit fortbendcountytx.gov for downloadable report
- Identify your property and available funds

### Step 2: Gather Required Documentation

**For Property Owners:**
- Deed showing ownership before judgment date
- Government-issued photo ID

**For Heirs:**
- Proof of relationship
- Probate records
- Death certificate

**For Lienholders:**
- Recorded lien documentation

### Step 3: Prepare Petition
- Draft petition for excess proceeds
- Include property details and cause number
- State your claim and relationship to property

### Step 4: File with Court
- Submit to Fort Bend County District Court
- File under original cause number if available
- Pay required filing fees

### Step 5: Serve All Parties
- Serve copy on all parties to underlying action
- Must serve at least 20 days before hearing
- Follow Texas Rules of Civil Procedure

### Step 6: Attend Hearing
- Present your documentation and claim
- Court determines distribution priority
- Receive court order directing payment

## DISTRIBUTION PRIORITY (Per Texas Tax Code)

Court orders payment in this priority:

1. **Tax sale purchaser** - if tax sale has been adjudged void
2. **Taxing units** - for taxes, penalties, or interest due after judgment
3. **Lienholders** - for amounts due under recorded liens
4. **Taxing units** - for unpaid taxes, penalties, interest in judgment
5. **Former owners** - as interests may appear

---

## IMPORTANT RESTRICTIONS

1. **Ownership Timing** - Must have owned property BEFORE judgment date
2. **2-Year Deadline** - Strictly enforced
3. **Proper Service Required** - Must serve all parties before hearing
4. **Documentation Required** - Must prove eligibility
