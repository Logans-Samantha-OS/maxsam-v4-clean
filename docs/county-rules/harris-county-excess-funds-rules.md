*Generated: January 20, 2026*

## OVERVIEW

Harris County (Houston area) handles excess funds through the District Clerk's Accounting Section. When property is sold at a tax foreclosure sale for more than the tax obligation, the surplus is deposited with the Accounting Section. Former owners are notified and have 2 years to claim the funds through a court petition.

## NOTIFICATION PROCESS

**Automatic Notification:** Upon receipt of excess proceeds greater than $25, a certified letter is sent to the former owner within 31 days of the sale.

---

## DEADLINE TO FILE CLAIM

**2 YEARS** from the date of the tax foreclosure sale.

If no petition is filed within this timeframe, funds are distributed to the taxing units.

---

## STEP-BY-STEP CLAIM PROCESS

### Step 1: Verify Excess Funds
- Email court.registry@hcdistrictclerk.com to request listing
- Note: Applicable fees may apply for excess proceeds listing
- Confirm amount and sale date

### Step 2: Gather Required Documentation
- Deed showing ownership before judgment date
- Government-issued photo ID
- Proof of current address
- Relationship documentation (if heir)

### Step 3: Prepare Court Petition
**Options:**
- Hire a private attorney, OR
- Visit Harris County Law Library for self-help resources

**Law Library Location:** 1019 Congress, downtown Houston

### Step 4: File Petition
- Submit to Harris County District Court
- File under original cause number if available
- Pay required filing fees

### Step 5: Obtain Court Order
The signed court order must contain:
- Former owner's name
- Former owner's address
- Exact amount of excess proceeds to release

### Step 6: Submit Order for Payment
- Present signed court order to Accounting Section
- Funds released per judge's order

## COURT ORDER REQUIREMENTS

The court order MUST include:
1. Former owner's legal name
2. Former owner's current address
3. Specific dollar amount to be released

**Without all three elements, the order cannot be processed.**

---

## IMPORTANT RESTRICTIONS

1. **Court Order Required** - Funds CANNOT be released without presiding judge's signature
2. **Ownership Timing** - Must have owned before judgment date
3. **Deadline Strictly Enforced** - No exceptions for late filings
4. **Fees May Apply** - For information requests
