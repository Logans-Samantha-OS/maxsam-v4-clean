## OVERVIEW

Denton County maintains a publicly available list of excess tax funds through the District Clerk's office. Sheriff's Sales are held on the first Tuesday of each month at the Denton County Courts Building. The county provides an online PDF document listing available excess funds, making it easier for claimants to identify potential claims.

## STEP-BY-STEP CLAIM PROCESS

### Step 1: Check Excess Funds List
- Visit dentoncounty.gov
- Download "Excess Tax Funds" PDF
- Search for your property or name
- Note cause number and amount

### Step 2: Gather Required Documentation

**For Property Owners:**
- Deed showing ownership before judgment date
- Government-issued photo ID

**For Lienholders:**
- Recorded lien documentation
- Lien release or proof of amounts owed

**For Heirs:**
- Proof of relationship (probate records)
- Death certificate
- Court documents establishing heirship

### Step 3: Prepare Petition
- Draft petition for excess proceeds
- Include property details, cause number, and claim amount
- State your relationship to property/owner

### Step 4: File with Court
- Submit to Denton County District Court
- File before 2nd anniversary of sale
- Pay required filing fees

### Step 5: Serve All Parties
- Serve copy on all parties to underlying action
- Must serve at least 20 days before hearing
- Follow Rule 21a, Texas Rules of Civil Procedure

### Step 6: Attend Hearing
- Present your case and documentation
- Court determines priority of claims
- Receive court order for distribution

## IMPORTANT RESTRICTIONS

1. **Post-Judgment Owners** - Cannot claim if acquired interest after judgment date
2. **Interest/Costs** - May NOT be allowed on claims
3. **2-Year Deadline** - Strictly enforced, no extensions
4. **Documentation Required** - Must prove eligibility with proper documents

## TIPS FOR SUCCESS

1. **Check PDF First** - Verify funds exist before starting process
2. **Note Cause Number** - You'll need this for all filings
3. **Act Early** - Don't wait until close to deadline
4. **Gather Documents First** - Probate records and deeds can take time
5. **Keep Copies** - Maintain copies of everything filed
